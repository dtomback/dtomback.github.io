He has a passion for our product, has been a New York Times subscriber for over 15 years, and has developed deep knowledge of affiliate marketing through his experience at Yipit, a start-up that curates and recommends the best online deals and offers for its users. He approaches both work and life with a team-first mentality and is dedicated to building, growing, and continuously improving products and services that make people's lives easier and more efficient.

Our pick
The best GM candidate for The Wirecutter
Dave Tomback
Dave Tomback's ability to both think strategically and manage effectively put him at the top of our list. His entrepreneurial spirit, experience managing an affiliate publisher with millions of users, and passion for both The Wirecutter and The New York Times makes him a natural fit.
Priceless

Dave is currently the General Manager of Yipit, a venture-backed company that curates and recommends the best deals and offers for its users based on their individual location and category preferences. Yipit earns affiliate revenue when its users purchase products and services through its distribution. As a result, he has extensive experience working directly with affiliate advertisers as well as with the major affiliate networks and outsourced program managers (OPMs).

Other people who applied for the position
While a number of other men and women would perform adequately, we feel that our top pick is far and away the best option. Dave would be a great addition to the team.

As GM, Dave oversees product, technology, marketing, and business operations. He is a data-driven operator that has learned to do a lot with a little. Dave is intellectually honest and adaptable but holds a number of deeply rooted philosophical beliefs. He believes that investments in user experience often result in the best business returns. He prefers an iterative approach to product development, testing key assumptions early to reduce risk and accelerate growth. He feels that a manager's job is to encourage ownership, not assigns tasks. He thinks that intelligence and drive often outweigh experience. He knows that growth does not have to come at the expense of efficiency.

These core beliefs have served him well. During the first year managing the business, the division doubled trailing twelve-month revenue and improved EBITDA margins from -23% to +52%. This was accomplished by adding new affiliate partners, improving existing affiliate partner terms, and introducing new products. The following year, the business maintained its top line and grew EBITDA 28% despite numerous major local deal and flash sale partners exiting the market. Dave focused on revenue diversification and expense management to keep the division profitable even though the industry was in steep decline.

Before his role expanded, Dave helped the division reduce data errors by 34% and content costs by 42% as the Head of Content. He automated content procurement and managed a dedicated 7-person team of outsourcers to improve accuracy and lower costs. Dave taught himself to code in order to do more on his own and so he could better communicate with the product and technology teams.

Prior to joining Yipit in 2011, Dave was a co-founder of neigh*borrow, a consumer internet company that facilitates local peer-to-peer sharing and renting of durable items needed only once or once in a while (e.g., drill, golf clubs, sewing machine, camping tent). Dave led business operations and managed non-technical aspects of product development, including wireframing, usability testing, web analytics, and customer development. Though Dave and his co-founder stopped working full-time on the concept, Dave credits this experience as his boot camp in learning how to take something from concept to reality.

Why you should trust him

Dave earned his MBA from Northwestern's Kellogg School of Management in 2006 and his undergraduate degree, with honors, from Tufts University in 1999. He has experience working for both big companies and small start-ups and currently serves as the General Manager of Yipit's Consumer Division where he's worked for over 5 years.

Why he loves The Wirecutter

Dave has been a reader of The Wirecutter for nearly 4 years after being introduced to the site by Yipit's CTO when shopping for a new television.

What he loves most about The Wirecutter and The Sweethome is that he trusts their process and therefore saves a lot of time on purchases both big and small. Left to his own devices, Dave would spend hours online researching the "best" this or that and still feel unsatisfied that he made the correct decision. He really appreciates the simplicity of a "this is our pick" approach combined with tremendous detail about that pick and the other products that were reviewed for those times he needs more background into the "why" behind the recommendation.

"These sites save me from falling into the rabbit hole of product reviews and give me back hours that can be used more productively."

He has personally relied on The Wirecutter and The Sweethome for product recommendations countless times. Most recently, Dave purchased REI's midweight men's top and bottom long underwear for an upcoming ski trip, the Jackery Bolt USB phone charger for his wife, the Honeywell HCM-350 humidifier for his daughter's room, the iRobot Roomba 650 to keep his apartment baby-crumb free, and the Chef'n FreshForce potato ricer to help him make fluffy, lump-free mashed potatoes for his first Thanksgiving-host experience. Unfortunately, the potato ricer does not help one determine the appropriate amount of butter and garlic for perfect potatoes. He should have checked NYT Cooking for that! In fact, The Wirecutter/Sweethome product recommendations would make for a great integration with NYT Recipes and Cooking Guides. There are many expansion and integration opportunities and Dave has a lot of ideas about how to prioritize and test the best options.

Dave has also referenced The Wirecutter and The Sweethome in his professional work. He regularly referenced and linked to the sites in the "Buy This, Not That!" section of the savings newsletter "One Stop Shop" that he launched at Yipit.

An example of the "Buy This, Not That!" section of Yipit's savings newsletter "One Stop Shop".

Why he loves The New York Times

Dave has been a NYT subscriber for nearly 15 years. For the last couple of years, he has gotten his daily news fix during the week from NYT online but still receives a physical paper on Saturdays and Sundays. He appreciates the break from his phone and computer to read all the news that's fit to print but could do without inky fingers.

Dave is a strong supporter of technological advancement and the massive benefits the internet has brought to him personally and to society more generally. That said, he is deeply troubled by the current popularity of sensationalized media and the thin reporting that has become so prevalent in today's world. He would like nothing more than to help an organization he believes in grow and adapt to the challenges it faces.

While no candidate is perfect in every way, we found that Dave's experience, attitude, creativity, and core principles best meshed with what we need to grow this business.
